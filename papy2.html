Post-AP: Software Engineering (24-25)

Course Description

The highly motivated students that take this advanced course gain a deeper understanding of the end-to-end process of developing software while executing independent projects in the domain of their choosing. This course also explores a range of fundamental Computer Science topics to help prepare them for further studies in Computer Science.

This course addresses three tracks in parallel: Software Engineering Topics, Core Computer Science Topics, and Independent Projects.

Software Engineering Topics

These are topics in Software Engineering that provide students a deeper understanding of the end-to-end process of developing software. These topics will enhance students’ ability to work with stakeholders and manage and deliver larger projects.

Topics to include:

  • System Planning
  • System Design
  • Testing
  • Project Management
Core Computer Science Topics

These are topics in Computer Science that provide a solid foundation of knowledge for students. Covering this broad range of topics will prepare students for further studies in Computer Science.

Topics to include:

  • Binary Number Systems
  • Binary Representation
  • Computer Architecture
  • Operating Systems
  • Virtual Memory
  • Compilers
  • Databases
  • Data Science
  • Networking
  • Web Architecture
  • Control Systems
Independent Projects

Students will propose and execute independent projects designed to advance their software engineering skills along with developing domain-specific knowledge.

Grading and Assessments

This course will use a range of assessments. These include written assessments and labs that all are designed to measure understanding and a student’s ability to apply their knowledge.

Learning Goals
  • Conceptual Understanding (25%)
    • Ability to demonstrate knowledge of course concepts
    • Ability to demonstrate knowledge of project domain

  • Technical Application (25%)
    • Ability to apply course concepts
    • Ability to apply knowledge of project domain

  • Design (25%)
    • Ability to apply the design process to project work

  • Communication (25%)
    • Ability to communicate technical concepts
    • Ability to communicate domain-specific concepts
